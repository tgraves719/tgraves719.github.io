Portraits of Change

The Roadmap

Phase 1

Portraits of Change Alpha

The Portraits of Change Alpha represents the major bootstrapping and rapid prototyping that took place before public performances. The different numerical versions of Portraits represent major breakthroughs in the software, and the log below represent different public tests that have been published online. We won’t have live performances until Phase 2, but we will have live demonstrations of our prototype using VR and a piano to see the wonders of 3D visualization and music.

Alpha 0.1.5

April was a busy time for Portraits of Change. We had made some huge breakthroughs in developing some kind of prototype of the initial idea of incorporating AR into a live music performance. MIDI turned out to be a key player in utilizing physical interfaces to generate low-latency effects directly from the music itself. It was a huge feat, but was a lot easier than we thought.

April 15th, 2019

Alpha 0.2.1

July Update- It has been a busy summer for Portraits of Change! We have officially reached our first fundraising milestone- to raise $1000! I just wanted take the time to thank all of our wonderful donors and supporters. This project would not be possible without your enthusiasm and passion for music.

We also have our first event finalized! On September 14th, 2019, you can find a booth of Portraits of Change at the What If... Festival of Innovation & Imagination in Downtown Colorado Springs! This is an amazing festival that celebrates lifelong learning and delights the community with the innovation, creativity, and the entrepreneurial spirit of the Pikes Peak region. More info coming soon!

July 11th, 2019

Alpha 0.2.2

Just a hint of what is coming with our Alpha 0.2.2 Booth Demo! New logo, new effects, new colors, new hands! Find us at the What If... Festival of Innovation and Imagination on September 14th in downtown Colorado Springs to check it out!

August 10th, 2019

Alpha 0.2.3

We have been hard at work testing our MIDI color system in augmented reality, while still polishing the VR experience for our upcoming events. We were fortunate to host our session pianist Noah Suazo for our most recent test, to uncover the possibilities of augmented reality! This test was used with the LIV software, and it is a really incredible tool for mixed reality recording. We are going to look into incorporating LIV into our live performances while we wait for the release of the Hololens 2.

August 25th, 2019

Alpha 0.2.4

With the What If... Festival wrapped up, we are bunkering down to develop our midi conversion system after all this time researching and prototyping on piano. 0.2.4 will see the adition of the trumpet along with bug fixes and updates to the performance mode of Porrtaits of Change. This will also be one of the last times we update the booth demo for a while.

2 down, 4 more to go!

Phase 2

Portraits of Change Beta

Once we have upgraded to Valve Basestation tracking with Vive trackers, we can support instrument presence and tracking of 6 different instruments with an hour-long setup and instant calibration. This is the only feasible way to perform Portraits live, since the current way we calibrate instrument presence in mixed reality takes several hours. We also need better music equipment for the MIDI conversion of our guitars and bass guitars. In terms of visuals, we are holding off any augmented reality HMD until Phase 3.

Phase 3

Portraits of Change 1.0

The great unknown. The frontier of audio-visual performance, being able to experience an improvised musical performance where you can see every note from every player, in 3D. We would hold small, intimate showcases with up to 12 participants. This would be the cutting edge of live performance and visual effects. 6 portraits that are painted through music, right in front of you.

Updated February 19, 2019

About us

Portraits of Change is an ongoing music-technology project that aims to cultivate jazz music through bold and unabashed innovation. It was created and developed by jazz musician and music student Thomas Graves. More information, including a full roster of artists and bios can be found here.
