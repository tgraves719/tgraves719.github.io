Portraits of Change

The Roadmap

An Overview

In brief overview, Portraits of Change can be split into 3 phases. Phase 1 consists of the alpha software, and is simply a proof of concept before we scale the Portraits prototype to more instruments/devices and create real performance opportunities for Phase 2. During this period, the theoretical beta version of the software will be compatible with augmented reality Head Mounted Displays but we will not have full fledged augmented reality shows until Phase 3. We’ve been in Phase 1 since January of 2019, and I predicted to arrive in Phase 2 sometime in 2020. We have been good with our internal timelines so far, but I can't put an estimate on Phase 3. Learn more about the different phases below, and check out the Dev Logs near the bottom for tests vids of our work in action!

Phase 1 (Current Phase)

Portraits of Change Alpha (0.1.0)-(0.X.X)

Since January of 2019, Portraits of Change has been built from the ground up. What started as a possible recital that focused on past innovations in jazz led to entirely new ways of seeing music. In March of 2019, Portraits of Change became a fiscally sponsored project through arts non-profit Fractured Atlas. This gave us the tools to thrive. The Portraits of Change Alpha represents the major bootstrapping and rapid prototyping that took place before public performances.

This alpha prototype was built using the Oculus CV1, LEAP motion sensor, various music equipment for MIDI conversion of live instruments, LIV software, and our own Unity software. This was the cheapest and one of the only options for mixed reality at the time this project started. VR has already grown commercially and we can leverage new technology to get to Phase 2.

Phase 2

Portraits of Change Beta (0.5.0)

Once we have upgraded to Valve Basestation tracking with Vive trackers, we can support 6 individually tracked instruments. This is the only feasible way to perform Portraits live, since the current way we calibrate instruments in mixed reality takes several hours. We also need new music equipment for the MIDI conversion of our guitars and bass guitars. In terms of visuals, we are holding off any augmented reality HMD until Phase 3. Phase 2 performances will feature LIV software and use projectors/TV screens at venues that support it. We will use a camera on stage in a tracked environment, and the camera can even move around on stage. The effects will be transposed on top using LIV, in real time.

Phase 3

Portraits of Change 1.0

The great unknown. The frontier of audio-visual performance, being able to experience an improvised concert where you can see every note from every player, in 3D. Maybe we could actually hire a unity developer and vfx artist to flesh the whole thing out. We would hold small, intimate showcases with around 12 participants. This would be the cutting edge of live performance and visual effects. 6 portraits that are painted through music, right in front of you with full on augmented reality. You arrive at a jazz club, put on some smart glasses and see galaxies form in front of you. You look at your hand, and browse information on the music you hear. We can use the same glasses to read sheet music and guide our effects, which will be totally unique each and every time you see it.

Dev Log

Alpha 0.1.5

April was a busy time for Portraits of Change. We had made some huge breakthroughs in developing some kind of prototype of the initial idea of incorporating AR into a live music performance. MIDI turned out to be a key player in utilizing physical interfaces to generate low-latency effects directly from the music itself. It was a huge feat, but was a lot easier than we thought.

April 15th, 2019

Alpha 0.2.1

July Update- It has been a busy summer for Portraits of Change! We have officially reached our first fundraising milestone- to raise $1000! I just wanted take the time to thank all of our wonderful donors and supporters. This project would not be possible without your enthusiasm and passion for music.

We also have our first event finalized! On September 14th, 2019, you can find a booth of Portraits of Change at the What If... Festival of Innovation & Imagination in Downtown Colorado Springs! This is an amazing festival that celebrates lifelong learning and delights the community with the innovation, creativity, and the entrepreneurial spirit of the Pikes Peak region. More info coming soon!

July 11th, 2019

Alpha 0.2.2

Just a hint of what is coming with our Alpha 0.2.2 Booth Demo! New logo, new effects, new colors, new hands! Find us at the What If... Festival of Innovation and Imagination on September 14th in downtown Colorado Springs to check it out!

August 10th, 2019

Alpha 0.2.3

We have been hard at work testing our MIDI color system in augmented reality, while still polishing the VR experience for our upcoming events. We were fortunate to host our session pianist Noah Suazo for our most recent test, to uncover the possibilities of augmented reality! This test was used with the LIV software, and it is a really incredible tool for mixed reality recording. We are going to look into incorporating LIV into our live performances while we wait for the release of the Hololens 2.

August 25th, 2019

Alpha 0.2.4

With the What If... Festival wrapped up, we are bunkering down to develop our MIDI conversion system after all this time researching and prototyping on piano. 0.2.4 will see the addition of the trumpet along with bug fixes and updates to the performance mode of Portraits of Change. This will also be one of the last times we update the booth demo for a while.

I really dislike how the trumpet module sounds in 0.2.4, but it is because we were using a practice mute with a mic inside to get our MIDI conversion. We will get a clip on mic and other music gear that will keep the original trumpet sound!

Alpha 0.2.5

This is where things get good. A little over a full year has past and we have a fully functioning trumpet module working! 0.2.5 will also be our first multi-instrument supported version.

I managed to get a recording with our session pianist Noah Suazo. It was a really great experience and really rich to finally see some multi instrument harmony in action. We are both energized to see what more of our music looks like!

About us

Portraits of Change is an ongoing music-technology project that aims to cultivate jazz music through bold and unabashed innovation. It was created and developed by jazz musician and music student Thomas Graves. More information, including a full roster of artists and bios can be found here.
